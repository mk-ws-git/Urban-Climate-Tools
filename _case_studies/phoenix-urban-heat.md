## Challenge

Phoenix experiences some of the highest urban heat in the United States, with temperatures regularly exceeding 110°F (43°C). The city's rapid growth and desert climate create significant heat-related health and energy challenges.

### The Solution

Phoenix implemented a multi-faceted heat mitigation strategy combining building retrofits, urban forestry expansion, and public health measures to protect residents and reduce energy consumption.

## Implementation

- **Cool Roofs Program**: Mandatory cool roof standards for new construction and incentives for retrofits
- **Urban Forestry Initiative**: Large-scale tree planting program targeting heat-vulnerable areas
- **Public Health Integration**: Heat alert systems and cooling centers for vulnerable populations

## Results

The comprehensive approach has significantly reduced urban heat, lowered energy demand, and improved public health outcomes in one of America's hottest cities.
