## Challenge

Paris experienced deadly heat waves in 2003 and 2019 that highlighted the city's vulnerability to extreme heat. Dense urban development, limited green space, and air pollution create significant health risks for residents.

### The Problem

- **Heat vulnerability**: 2003 heat wave caused thousands of deaths
- **Low canopy cover**: Dense historic city with limited tree coverage
- **Air pollution**: Traffic and urban activity impact air quality
- **Biodiversity decline**: Urbanization has reduced habitat

## Solution Approach

Paris launched an ambitious urban forestry program as part of its climate action plan, committing to plant 170,000 trees and create new green spaces throughout the city.

### 1. Strategic Tree Planting

Using i-Tree tools to identify priority locations based on heat vulnerability, air quality, and community access to green space.

### 2. Urban Forest Development

Creating four new urban forests on major sites, including near City Hall and the Opera, transforming hardscape into green space.

### 3. Schoolyard Transformation

Converting asphalt schoolyards into green oases, providing cooling for children and neighborhoods.

### 4. Corridor Connections

Linking green spaces through tree-lined corridors to support biodiversity movement and create continuous shade networks.

## Key Outcomes

### Environmental Impact
- **170,000 trees** planted or committed
- **14% canopy coverage** achieved
- **1.2M tons CO2/year** sequestered by urban forest
- **Temperature reduction**: 3-5C cooling in greened areas

### Health Impact
- **Air quality improvement**: Reduced particulate matter in greened areas
- **Heat resilience**: More shade and cooling for vulnerable populations
- **Mental health**: Increased access to green space

### Biodiversity Impact
- **Habitat creation**: New ecosystems in urban forests
- **Pollinator support**: Native plantings support bees and butterflies
- **Wildlife corridors**: Connected green spaces enable movement

## Lessons Learned

1. **Bold commitments drive action**: 170,000 tree goal mobilized resources
2. **Multi-benefit approach**: Trees address heat, air quality, and biodiversity simultaneously
3. **Priority setting matters**: Focus on vulnerable neighborhoods maximizes impact
4. **Maintenance is critical**: Long-term care ensures tree survival

## Replication Potential

Paris's model demonstrates how historic, dense cities can significantly increase green space. The schoolyard transformation program is particularly transferable, as most cities have school grounds that could be greened.
