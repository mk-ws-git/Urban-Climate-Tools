## Challenge

Bogota, one of Latin America's largest cities, faces complex climate challenges intertwined with poverty and inequality. Informal settlements on hillsides are vulnerable to landslides, while water scarcity and air pollution affect all residents.

### The Problem

- **Climate vulnerability**: Informal settlements at high risk
- **Water challenges**: Seasonal scarcity and flooding
- **Air pollution**: Traffic congestion impacts health
- **Social inequality**: Climate impacts fall hardest on poorest residents

## Solution Approach

Bogota developed an integrated approach that addresses climate adaptation alongside poverty reduction and social inclusion, recognizing that climate action must benefit all residents.

### 1. Green Corridors

Created 50km of green corridors connecting parks and natural areas, providing cooling, biodiversity habitat, and recreational access for underserved neighborhoods.

### 2. Sustainable Transportation

Expanded the TransMilenio bus rapid transit system and developed extensive cycling infrastructure, reducing emissions while improving mobility for all income levels.

### 3. Community Resilience

Invested in community capacity building, training 20,000 residents in climate adaptation, emergency response, and sustainable practices.

### 4. Informal Settlement Upgrading

Improved infrastructure in vulnerable hillside communities, reducing landslide risk while improving quality of life.

## Key Outcomes

### Environmental Impact
- **50km green corridors** created
- **Air quality improvement** through transit investments
- **Urban cooling** from expanded green infrastructure
- **Watershed protection** through upstream conservation

### Social Impact
- **150 new transit routes** improving mobility
- **20,000 residents** trained in climate adaptation
- **Reduced inequality** in access to green space and transit
- **Community resilience** in vulnerable neighborhoods

### Economic Impact
- **Green jobs** created in corridor maintenance
- **Reduced commute costs** for low-income residents
- **Property value stabilization** in improved neighborhoods

## Lessons Learned

1. **Climate and equity together**: Integrated approach multiplies benefits
2. **Community leadership**: Local organizations essential to success
3. **Transit as climate action**: Sustainable mobility reduces emissions and improves access
4. **Green infrastructure for all**: Ensure benefits reach underserved areas

## Replication Potential

Bogota's model is highly relevant for other developing world cities facing similar challenges. The integration of climate action with poverty reduction and the emphasis on community engagement are transferable principles.
