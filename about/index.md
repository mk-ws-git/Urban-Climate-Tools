        <div class="about-section">
            <h2 class="about-section__heading">Our Mission</h2>
            <p class="about-section__body">Climate change poses an existential challenge to cities worldwide. Rising temperatures, more frequent flooding, coastal erosion, and worsening air quality are reshaping urban life. Yet the tools to confront these challenges already exist.</p>
            <p class="about-section__body">Our mission is to make climate adaptation accessible and actionable by connecting people with the world's best open-source and evidence-based tools. We believe that the right tool, in the right hands, at the right moment can meaningfully change outcomes for communities and ecosystems alike.</p>
        </div>

            <h2 class="about-section__heading">How to Use This Site</h2>

            <div class="about-steps">
                <div class="about-step">
                    <div class="about-step__num">1</div>
                    <div>
                        <p class="about-step__title">Find Your Tools</p>
                        <p class="about-step__body">Start with the <a href="{{ '/quiz/' | relative_url }}">Tool Finder Quiz</a> for a personalised match, or go to <a href="{{ '/tools/' | relative_url }}">Browse Tools</a> and use filters for category, cost, skill level, continent, and user type. Each tool page includes a full description, key features, data requirements, and direct links to documentation.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="about-step">
                    <div class="about-step__num">2</div>
                    <div>
                        <p class="about-step__title">Compare Options</p>
                        <p class="about-step__body">Use the <a href="{{ '/compare/' | relative_url }}">Tool Comparator</a> to place two tools side by side and evaluate their cost, skill requirements, coverage, and use cases. You can also open the comparator directly from any tool page.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="about-step">
                    <div class="about-step__num">3</div>
                    <div>
                        <p class="about-step__title">Learn from Case Studies</p>
                        <p class="about-step__body">Browse the <a href="{{ '/case-studies/' | relative_url }}">Case Studies</a> section or open the <a href="{{ '/map/' | relative_url }}">Interactive Map</a> to explore how cities worldwide have applied these tools. Filter by challenge type, continent, or user type to find the most relevant examples.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="about-step">
                    <div class="about-step__num">4</div>
                    <div>
                        <p class="about-step__title">Explore Climate Data</p>
                        <p class="about-step__body">Head to the <a href="{{ '/data/' | relative_url }}">Climate Data Explorer</a> to search any city and pull live temperature trends, precipitation, and heat-stress indicators, powered by the free Open-Meteo API. Charts can be used in reports, presentations, or academic work.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="about-step">
                    <div class="about-step__num">5</div>
                    <div>
                        <p class="about-step__title">Save Items to Your Profile</p>
                        <p class="about-step__body">Click the bookmark icon on any tool or case study card to save it. Your saved items are stored locally in your browser by default — no account needed. To sync across devices, use the <strong>Sign In</strong> button (top right) to create a free account or sign in with Google. Your saves are then accessible from your profile menu at any time.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

Head to <a href="{{ '/contact/#submit' | relative_url }}">Submit a Resource</a>, or open a pull request on <a href="https://github.com/mk-ws-git/Urban-Climate-Tools">GitHub</a>.
