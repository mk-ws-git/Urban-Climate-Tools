## Overview

The LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) Rating System is the market-leading voluntary certification framework establishing performance standards for new construction, existing buildings, and interior fit-outs. Administered by the U.S. Green Building Council, LEED defines measurable criteria across site selection, energy and water efficiency, materials sourcing, indoor environmental quality, and resilience—enabling developers and owners to demonstrate sustainability commitment and access market premiums.

## Key Features

- **Credit-based rating structure**: Point allocation across multiple categories including Energy Performance, Water Efficiency, Materials & Resources, and Indoor Environmental Quality
- **Tiered certification levels**: Certified, Silver, Gold, and Platinum awards based on total points achieved, enabling market differentiation
- **Energy and water benchmarks**: Prescriptive and performance-path compliance options tied to ASHRAE and EPA standards
- **Resilience and equity criteria**: Credits for flood resistance, heat mitigation design, and equitable community benefit in recent rating versions

## Who Is It For?

Project developers, design teams, property managers, and building owners seeking third-party verification of sustainability performance. LEED suits advanced practitioners; certification projects typically engage specialized consultants and commissioning agents to navigate compliance requirements across design, construction, and operation phases.

## Getting Started

Visit [new.usgbc.org/leed](https://new.usgbc.org/leed) to review rating systems appropriate to your project type (New Construction, Existing Buildings, Interior, Neighbourhood Development). Register with USGBC to access credit guidance documents, reference standards, and the Project Registration Portal. Professional training and credential exams (LEED AP) prepare team members to guide compliance from design through certification.
