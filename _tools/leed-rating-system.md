## Overview

LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is the world's most widely used green building rating system. Developed by the U.S. Green Building Council, LEED provides a framework for healthy, highly efficient, and cost-saving green buildings.

### Key Features

- **Rating Categories**: Building Design, Operations, Interior Design, Neighborhood Development, Cities
- **Credit System**: Points-based certification levels (Certified, Silver, Gold, Platinum)
- **Performance Tracking**: Ongoing monitoring through LEED Arc
- **Global Standards**: Recognized in over 180 countries
- **Resilience Credits**: New pilot credits for climate adaptation

## Use Cases

- New building design and construction
- Existing building operations and maintenance
- Interior fit-outs and tenant improvements
- Neighborhood and community development
- City-wide sustainability programs

## Getting Started

1. Visit [USGBC LEED](https://new.usgbc.org/leed)
2. Select the appropriate rating system
3. Register your project
4. Work through prerequisite and credit requirements
5. Submit for certification review

## Pricing

- Registration fees vary by project type and size
- Certification review fees apply
- LEED Green Associate and AP credentials available
